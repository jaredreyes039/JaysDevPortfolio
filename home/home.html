ABOUT ME

Hi, my name is Jay, a pre-engineering major and fresh face in the full-stack web development scene! With over 1 year of experience working with static and dymamic site development, I've discovered a love for the power of coding and programming. Having developed severe agoraphobia in early 2020 (Thanks Covid), I had to find a new way to live my life and feel the same freedom I felt before- and that's when I found coding. Beginning my journey in Python through the FreeCodeBootCamp, I quickly jumped into front-end development with the "Big 3" to bring my site ideas to life, and lastly found myself in the depths of full-stack development as I began to experiment with MERN and PHP. Where these depths will lead, I will never know, but I am always excited for the adventure along the way!

BUILDING SITES TO MEET YOUR COMPANY NEEDS

SIMPLE

While I am always excited for new challenges, I remember wise words from an old history teacher,
"Keep it simple, stupid!"
I am consistently looking for more than just solutions, but solutions at their simplest level of implementation possible. I remember very well being a confused engineering student trying to learn MATLAB and going a bit insane; therefore, my coding solutions are intended to be as simple as possible for developers and non-developers alike.

TRENDY

What good is a site without the latest frontend and backend tools that bring that site to life? With a particular interest in animations and working with 3D front-end displays, giving life to a site brings me so much joy- and believe me- there is NEVER a dull moment! I've made it a personal mission to continuously study up the latest trends in JS, Node, React, etc. to find new and improved ways of implementing fantastic, dynamic features to my projects!

CLEAN

Clean code is crucial to maintaining a long-lasting, ever-updating code architecture. As a cornerstone of web development, I take pride in striving towards clean programming and coding practices and adhering to an organization method that makes it easy for fellow developers to understand my process. Also, regarding bugs, consider me an entomophobic- I am not fond of them by any means, and I will scrub every inch of my code to get them away!
